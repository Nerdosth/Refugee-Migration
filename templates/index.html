Refugee Migration Trends & Predictions

Project Background

Our project aims to analyze data provided by the United Nations Refugee Agency (UNHCR) and other sources to gain insights into migration patterns and to develop a machine learning model to focus on refugee migration. The data includes information on refugee populations, demographic information (gender & age), asylum decisions, conflict and disasters impacting refugee populations around the world. We hope to gain a deeper understanding of the factors that influence migration flows and develop predictions of future migration patterns.

Project Summary

In the past decade, the global refugee crisis has more than doubled in scope. In 2022, the UNHCR announced that we had surpassed the 100 million mark for total displaced persons, meaning that over 1.2% of the global population have been forced to leave their homes. Among these people are over 32.5 million refugees. 76% of those refugees come from just six countries. Machine Learning

The employed Decision Tree Classifier machine learning model yielded a precision-weighted average score of 63%, which was improved by refining the input data to include the top 20 countries of origin and their corresponding top 20 countries of asylum. While machine learning models can be useful, they may not fully capture the complexities and uncertainties of refugee migration, which is influenced by political instability (war), human rights violations, environmental disasters, and economic hardship. Therefore, machine learning models should not be the sole or primary source of information for decision-making on refugee migration policies and interventions.
